# **🟢 Level 1 - Chat — your starting point**

Ask questions, get research, brainstorm ideas. This is the foundation. Start here — but don't stay here.

**How to set up**

**1 Go to claude.ai**

Open your browser and visit claude.ai. Click "Sign up" and create a free account with your email or Google.

**2 Start a new chat**

Click "New chat" in the left sidebar. Type your question or task in the message box and press Enter.

claude.ai → New chat

**3 Enable web search**

Click the search icon in the chat toolbar to turn on live web search. Now Claude pulls from real-time sources too.

Chat toolbar → Search icon

**4 Upload files if needed**

Click the paperclip icon to attach PDFs, images, or docs. Claude reads and analyses them in the same chat.

**Key features at this level**

!Screenshot 2026-04-29 at 8.30.50 AM.png

**Try these prompts**

**Research**

"Summarise the top 3 trends in AI marketing for 2026 with sources"

**Brainstorm**

"Give me 10 content ideas for a LinkedIn post about productivity for founders"

**Advice**

"What questions should I ask before hiring a freelance designer?"

Hack: Be specific. Instead of "write me an email", try "write a follow-up email to a client who hasn't responded in 5 days — keep it short and friendly, with a clear next step.”

# **🟢 Level 2 - Context — teach Claude your world**

Upload your SOPs, brand docs, and project files into Claude Projects. Every answer now fits your business — not a generic one.

**How to set up Claude Projects**

**1 Open Projects in the sidebar**

On claude.ai, look for "Projects" in the left sidebar. Click it, then click "New project" and give it a name (e.g. "My Business").

claude.ai → Projects → New project

**2 Upload your knowledge files**

Inside the project, click "Add content" or the upload icon. Add your SOPs, brand guidelines, tone of voice doc, product info, pricing, FAQs — anything Claude should always know.

Project → Add content → Upload files

**3 Write your project instructions**

Click "Project instructions" and type a brief about who you are, your business, your tone, and any rules Claude should always follow. This runs in the background of every chat in this project.

Project → Project instructions

**4 Start every task inside this project**

Click "New chat" from within your project — not from the sidebar. Claude now has your full context loaded automatically.

**What to upload**

!Screenshot 2026-04-29 at 8.31.53 AM.png

**Example project instruction**

**Paste this into your project instructions and edit it**

"You are helping [Your Name], founder of [Brand]. Our tone is direct, warm, and expert — never corporate or fluffy. Always write in British English. Our audience is small business owners aged 28–45. When writing content, match the examples in the uploaded brand doc. Never add emojis unless I ask."

Hack: Create separate projects for separate clients or topics — e.g. one for "Content creation", one for "Client A", one for "Operations". Each project keeps its own memory and files.

# **🟢 Level 3 - Real work — Claude as your teammate**

Connect Claude to your actual email, calendar, and tools. Stop copy-pasting. Claude reads, drafts, and plans directly inside your workflow.

**How to connect your tools**

**1 Open Integrations in Settings**

Click your profile icon (bottom left) → Settings → Integrations. This is where you connect external apps to Claude.

Profile → Settings → Integrations

**2 Connect Google or Microsoft**

Click "Connect" next to Gmail, Google Calendar, or Microsoft 365. Sign in and grant read access. Claude can now see your emails and schedule.

**3 Tell Claude what you need**

In your project chat, describe the real task. Claude reads your actual data and produces the output. You review and approve — nothing sends automatically.

**4 Build a daily briefing habit**

Start each morning with a single prompt that asks Claude to scan your inbox, check your calendar, and give you a day plan. Takes 30 seconds and saves an hour.

**What Claude can do at this level**

!Screenshot 2026-04-29 at 8.32.46 AM.png

**Real-world prompts**

**Email**

"Check my inbox and draft replies to emails waiting more than 3 days. Use my usual tone."

**Calendar**

"Look at my calendar this week. Spot any back-to-back meetings and suggest a better structure with breaks."

**Meeting notes**

"Here are my rough notes from today's client call. Turn them into a clean action plan with owners and deadlines."

Morning briefing prompt: "Check my emails from the last 24 hours and my calendar for today. Give me: 3 things that need urgent replies, my schedule in order, and one thing I should clear before noon."

# **🟢 Level 4 -Build — create things without coding**

Websites, tools, automations, prototypes. Describe what you want in plain English. Claude writes the code and you ship it — no developer needed.

**Two ways to build with Claude**

**1 Option A — Build inside claude.ai (no setup)**

In any chat, ask Claude to create something. It will generate code and display it as a live "Artifact" on the right panel — you can see and interact with it immediately.

Chat → describe your build → Artifact panel appears

**2 Option B — Use Claude Code (for bigger builds)**

For full apps and multi-file projects, install Claude Code — a terminal tool or VS Code extension. It understands your entire project and edits files directly.

claude.ai/code → Install Claude Code

**3 Describe your build clearly**

The more detail you give, the better the output. Describe the purpose, who uses it, what it should do, and any style preferences. You don't need technical knowledge.

**4 Iterate with plain English**

After the first build, just say what you want changed. "Make the button blue", "Add a contact form", "Make it mobile-friendly." Claude updates it each time.

**What you can build**

!Screenshot 2026-04-29 at 8.34.13 AM.png

**Build prompts to try**

**Landing page**

"Build me a landing page for a coaching offer. Headline, 3 benefits, one testimonial, a CTA button. Minimal, modern style."

**Form / tool**

"Create a client onboarding form that collects: name, business type, main goal, budget range, and how they found me."

**Automation**

"Write a Google Apps Script that sends a follow-up email 3 days after a new row is added to my Google Sheet."

Hack: Paste a screenshot of a website you like and say "build me something similar for my [business type]." Claude will match the layout and style, then you customise from there

# **🟢 Level 5 -Systems — run on autopilot**

Stop working task by task. Build repeatable workflows, saved prompt libraries, and scheduled tasks that run without you triggering them.

**Three tools at Level 5**

**A Prompt library — build once, reuse forever**

Inside any Project, save your best prompts as reusable templates. Write it once, use it daily with one click. No retyping, consistent output every time.

Project → Instructions → save your top 10 prompts as templates

**B Scheduled tasks — Claude runs on a timer**

In claude.ai, look for "Scheduled tasks" or use the Cowork desktop app. Set Claude to run tasks at set times — daily briefing, weekly report, content batch — without you opening the app.

claude.ai → Explore → Scheduled tasks

**C Claude Cowork — your desktop AI agent**

Download Claude Cowork (desktop app). It can work on your actual files, open apps, move data, and complete multi-step tasks on your computer. You describe the goal — it handles the steps.

claude.ai → Apps → Download Cowork

**Your prompt library — start here**

Weekly content batch

"Using my brand guidelines and tone doc, write 5 LinkedIn posts for this week. Topics: [paste topics]. Each post should be under 150 words, end with a question or CTA."

**Morning briefing**

"Check my inbox and calendar. Give me: top 3 urgent replies needed, today's schedule, and one thing I should prioritise before noon."

**Weekly report**

"Pull this week's key updates from [tool/doc]. Write a 1-page summary report: what happened, what worked, what needs attention next week."

**Client proposal**

"Using my SOP and pricing doc, write a proposal for [client name]. They need [service]. Tone: professional but warm. Include scope, timeline, investment, and next steps."
